Artificial Intelligence for Stylus Engine

Authors: Mark Boudreau, Christian Hains, Quinn Shavey(and associates)

Abstract

Stylus Engine is a program created in 2008 by Dr. Douglas Axe to simulate evolution using Chinese characters to represent genomes made up of strokes, that represent amino acids, following a vector-based drawing system based on the 3-letter format of DNA sequences. Strokes are the lines that make up the character. Genomes (complete chinese characters) would go through mutations (vector-altering algorithms), after which being given a score, which would be assigned based on how close it was to the original genome in vector orientation. Originally, the program had to be fed a genome image, as well as the geneome name, after which, it would evaluate the score. Our goal was to create an Artificial Intelligence that would identify a character, in order that we could then evaluate the score of a genome off of only the image, without knowing the genome name.

Methods

We used a dual-branch CNN,Dia. 1 (convolutionary neural net), which is known to work well with character recognition projects with a second layer, like stroke recognition, or font. Most models aren't built for a form of analysis like this, which is why dual branch CNN was our choice.

Training an AI requires a large amount of data, with which we used a three-set mutation,Dia. 2 in which we made ~12 million genomes.

After generating the genes, we trained our neural network using a Google Colab A100, and we evaluated the final model based off of genomes that it did NOT train with, to make sure that we avoided memorization.

Results

The final model achieved a high degree of accuracy. Below are the evaluation metrics on the test dataset:

Limitations

  • Time & data: more time to create more training data would have created a much more successful AI.
  • Lower limit: we limited the score of the characters fed to the AI so that if the genomes were too bad, we would not train with them. This makes our AI unable to identify lower score characters. Ideally, there should be edge cases for low and high score characters.
  • Stroke count: we trained the AI to work on specifically 6-stroke genomes, so it can only identify those that have 6 strokes.

Next Steps

  • Time & data: We will train our AI with more data, to make it even more accurate in the future.
  • Lower limit: Ideally, we want to have an AI that has enough data with low score genomes to identify the ones that have low scores.
  • Stroke count: We will have a master program which counts the strokes in the chinese characters, then routes the task image to the specialized AI trained with its corresponding stroke count.
